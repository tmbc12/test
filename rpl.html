Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

The Government of India's Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE) launched the Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) initiative. It aims to certify the skills of the large, uncertified workforce, aligning them with the National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF). This certification equates informal learning with formal education across various sectors.

Objectives of RPL:

  • Align pre-existing workforce competencies with the NSQF.

  • Enhance individual employability.

  • Reduce inequalities by valuing all forms of knowledge.

  • Increase industry acceptance of the certification.

RPL Certification Process:

  • Mobilization & Pre-Screening

  • Orientation

  • Final Assessment

  • Certification

Any Candidates with an AADHAAR Card and relevant prior learning or experience are eligible.

Why RPL?

Without recognized qualifications, many individuals struggle to secure decent jobs, migrate, or access further education despite having the necessary skills. RPL provides formal qualifications, improving employability, mobility, lifelong learning, social inclusion, and self-esteem.

Benefits of RPL for Employers:

  • Recognize and boost the morale of employees without formal certifications.

  • Identify suitable training opportunities.

  • Establish objective skill assessments based on the NSQF.

  • Align RPL assessments with internal performance evaluations to inform learning and development decisions.

  • Increase employee retention and reduce recruitment and training costs.
